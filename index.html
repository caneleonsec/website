Attack Scenario: An attacker attemps to attack CAN0 from PC.

Videos of Self-Driving Vehicle

Normal Trajectory Following

The normal run is consists of three parts:

  1. The initial stage the vehicle accel- erated to a target speed, then maintain the current speed until it entered the circle.
  2. The apex stage the vehicle steered and reduced the velocity to complete a big U-turn.
  3. The returning state the vehicle com- pleted the U-turn, accelerated and finally slowed down at the end.
Stopped by CAN bus attacks

In this attack scenario, fabricated 0x184h frames were injected from the PC side at about 4s after the car started, which deceived the target ECU that the ‘expected’ velocity is 0 m/s.

Without CANeleon, the gateway forwarded all those frames to ECUs. Consequently, the vehicle velocity quickly reduced and the car finally stopped, leaving the self-driving system desperately trying to move it forward.

CAN bus revived from attack

with CANeleon enabled on both the PC and gateway, the system would successfully mitigate the attack.

After the attack was identified, both the PC and the gateway quickly switched from 0x184h to 0x17Fh to continue sending the commands to the ECU, and the gateway interpreted each 0x17Fh and sent it to CAN1 as 0x184h frames.

As a result, the vehicle was able to fully complete the normal trajectory, although with a slight delay due to the stop.
